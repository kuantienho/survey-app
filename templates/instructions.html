Welcome to the “AI-Assisted Ideation through Contrary and Base-Additive Combinational Framework” Research Experiment 1

Thank you for participating. You belong to Group A (Idea Generation). This study examines how people generate ideas by combining a Base (the main object or concept) with an Additive (a supporting or stimulating concept), and occasionally applying Contrary (opposite) thinking to open up new directions. We use an AI-assisted workflow that suggests potential Additives alongside a Base, to see whether this can broaden ideation paths and support clarity and diversity in ideas.

Background & Example

In creative design, we often start from an existing concept (Base) and combine it with something new or unexpected (Additive). For example, a Japanese inventor imagined a new kind of cutting tool not by referencing typical knives, but by combining a razor (Base) with stripes of chocolate bars (Additive) — a surprising pairing that led to a unique shape and use.

(Image placeholder: Razor + Chocolate Bar → Cutting Knife)

This story illustrates that—even though everyone knows what a chocolate bar is—we may not recall it when designing a new tool. Such “potential but hard-to-recall Additives” can be crucial creative triggers. To support this, our study introduces an AI-assisted Additive suggestion workflow that presents candidate Additives alongside each Base.

Your Role (Group A)

As a member of Group A (Idea Generation), you will complete several independent idea-generation tasks. Each task begins with a presented Base, and you will create one idea by following the on-screen instructions. Instructions may differ between tasks, but no prior knowledge or special preparation is required. The detailed procedure for Experiment 1 appears on the next page.
